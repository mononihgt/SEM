Questions

  • What are the components of the congeneric model of measurement?
    • Observations; Common Factor; Factor loading; Error Terms
  • Which parameter is only specific for the item
    • Factor loading (λ)
  • What is the major difference between the congeneric model and the bifactor model
    • Congeneric model: It assumes that all measurement items measure the same latent construct, but allows different items to have different factor loadings and different error terms. It does not assume that there is a general factor that affects all measurement items, but allows correlations between items.
    • Bifactor model: It contains a general factor that has direct loadings on all observed variables. In addition, there are several grouping factors that only load on subsets of observed variables. A key feature of the bifactor model is that these grouping factors are orthogonal (i.e., uncorrelated) with the general factor.

Lecture 5 Exploratory Factor Analysis

Summary

  • Basic Concepts
    • item: a denotation that is used in assessment and exploratory factor analysis for questions / statement / task of a test and a questionnaire
    • factor: is a dimensions that characterizes a set of items
    • the basic idea of explorative factor analysis: search for (underlying) dimensions
  • Fundamental theorem
    • A standardized score is described as a linear combination of factor scores, of their weights and an error component
  • Communality estimation
    • estimation of explained variance
  • Factor interpretation
    • find the common categories for factors

Questions

  • What are the purposes for selecting FA?
    • Search for structure; Data reduction; Check for assumed structure; Construction of questionnaire
  • What type of variance is represented by the communality
    • true(systematic) variance of the item
  • Why is factor rotation conducted
    • The results of factor extraction usually leads to a bad interpretation; The literal aim of rotation is the achievment of factors that enable a "good" interpretation
    • Formal goal of factor rotation(criterion): simple structure of the pattern of factor loadings
  • What is the principle of factor rotation
    • each variable should show a large loading on one factor only and small loading on the other factors
    • each factor should have several high loadings
